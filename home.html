Our Electric Categories

Battery Electric Vehicles (BEVs):

Explanation: BEVs are fully electric vehicles that rely solely on a battery to store and deliver energy to power an electric motor. They have no internal combustion engine and produce zero tailpipe emissions. BEVs are charged by plugging into an electrical outlet or charging station.

Plug-in Hybrid Electric Vehicles

Explanation: PHEVs combine an internal combustion engine with an electric motor and a battery. They can run on electricity alone for a limited range, after which the internal combustion engine kicks in. PHEVs can also be charged using an electrical outlet or charging station, providing flexibility between electric and gasoline power.

Hybrid Electric Vehicles (HEVs):

Explanation: HEVs have both an internal combustion engine and an electric motor but cannot be charged externally. They use regenerative braking to recharge the battery and improve fuel efficiency by switching between the two power sources as needed. HEVs do not produce tailpipe emissions while running on electricity.

Rideshare and Micromobility Electric Vehicles

Explanation: Some rideshare companies and micromobility services offer electric options for short trips within cities. These include electric scooters, bikes, and small electric cars for rent by the minute or hour.
